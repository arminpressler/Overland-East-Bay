Gaia GPS Tips & Tricks

Essential navigation strategies for overlanding

For more navigation tips and tools, see the Navigation Tools page

Gaia GPS is one of the most powerful navigation tools for overlanding. Here are essential tips and tricks to get the most out of it for your adventures.

Essential Map Strategies

Layer Multiple Maps for Complete Information

Toggle between different map layers like Gaia Overland, NatGeo Trails Illustrated, USFS maps, and MVUM to get a comprehensive picture of trail conditions, road closures, and terrain. Each map type reveals different information that others may miss.

Download Maps Before You Go

Always download your maps for offline use before heading out. Gaia Overland is designed with file efficiency so you can download huge areas in minutes without hogging phone storage. Download your entire route or even your entire state.

Best Map Layers for Overlanding

  • Gaia Overland: Your primary all-in-one map with roads, trails, public land boundaries, MVUM data, and BLM routes
  • Private Land overlay: Prevents trespassing by showing land ownership boundaries
  • Satellite Imagery: Helps scout dispersed campsites and secondary trails
  • Cell Coverage map: Shows where you'll have service in the US

Trip Planning Pro Tips

Plan Multiple Camping Options

For longer trips, map out three different camping spots: one if you need to stop early, your planned camp, and one if you're ahead of schedule. Also mark exit points to main roads for emergencies.

Weather and Conditions

Use precipitation layers and snow forecast maps to determine if road conditions will be problematic. Weather significantly affects trail surfaces, especially at different elevations.

Create Waypoints On-The-Go

Tap the (+) symbol to quickly add waypoints for camping spots, interesting features, or hazards you discover. You can attach photos to waypoints to document conditions.

Navigation Features

Apple CarPlay & Android Auto

Connect Gaia GPS to your vehicle's dashboard screen for easier navigation. You can run it on both your dash and phone simultaneously for different zoom levels.

Snap-to-Trail Routing

Use the smart snap-to-trail tool for rerouting on the fly and exploring side trails without losing your way back.

Organize with Folders

Create dedicated folders for each trip containing all related tracks, routes, and waypoints. This keeps your data organized and easy to access.

Import and Share

Import Existing Tracks

Use the Trails Offroad integration to push detailed trail guides directly to your Gaia GPS account with one button. Many groups and users share their recorded tracks.

Key Reminders

  • Get Gaia GPS Premium to unlock the full map catalog and offline capabilities ($40/year)
  • Study maps on the desktop at gaiagps.com before your trip for better planning
  • Check the current Wildfires layer before heading out, as it updates daily
  • Points of interest include gas stations, springs, scenic overlooks, and mountain passes with detailed information
