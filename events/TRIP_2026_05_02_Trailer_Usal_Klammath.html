Klamath River

We will also be camping along the Klamath River. Either way, we'll spend the majority of the next week at Klamath, using it as a base for hikes and sights in that area.

We'll stay on the river east of town, lots of room and the people are very welcoming. Plenty of room on a 4-6 inch river rock bar, unless we get late spring rain, but even then there's room up a bit.

Tons of activities:

  • Fern Canyon
  • Newton B Drury Redwood Scenic Parkway hikes (lots)
  • Klamath overlook
  • Ladybird Johnson Grove
  • Grove of Titans
  • North Fork of the Smith (secret spot, alas, not big enough for us all at once)
  • Even a further camp to the north if we have a couple of folks looking to explore (will require chainsaws and probably winches), but allegedly beautiful on a gravel bar.
  • Secret WWII military installation
  • Blue Creek Ah Pah
  • Sue-meg State Park
  • More...
